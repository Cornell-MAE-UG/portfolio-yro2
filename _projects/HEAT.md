As part of the MAE 3240 course (Heat Transfer), one of our homework assignments focuses on analyzing the thermal performance of a 5×5 pin fin heat sink array and the transient cooling of an electrical wire submerged in an oil bath. These types of analyses are critical in the development of cooling systems for power electronics and renewable energy infrastructure, where maintaining stable operating temperatures is essential for efficiency and safety. The objective of this work is to model heat dissipation through fin arrays, derive the steady-state and transient temperature profiles of current-carrying components, and evaluate the effectiveness of various heat sink configurations.
