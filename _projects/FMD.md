As part of the MAE 3230 course (Introductory Fluid Mechanics), this project focuses on performing a dissection of a fluid mechanical device. My project group was assigned a simple but interesting machine: the humidifier. Working together, we carefully dissected the machine in a laboratory, each offering theories for the function of the various parts. We worked through this process as a team, but my focus was particularly on the mechanism that was allowing the atomization of the water. Namely, I looked into how the rapid spinning disk could serve a dual purpose of being a turbomachine funneling air into the humidifier, while also causing water to disperse at high speeds into tiny droplets. We developed a video explaining our findings and analysis of the humidifier. My contribution to the video including dicussing and writing the script, setting up filming and the background, and talking about the process of atomizing the water in the video itself. The process was largely a group effort, and each one of my team members made great contributions to our analysis and video.
